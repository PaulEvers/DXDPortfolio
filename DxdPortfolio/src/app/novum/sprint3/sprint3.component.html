Sprint 3 - Concepting

Creating prototypes and testing them with our audience.

Introduction

Now that in the previous sprint we have more clearly defined the problems our audience faces, we can start working on coming up with concepts and prototypes. During this sprint we decided to focus on low-fidelity prototypes and test them with our audience. The insights we would get from these would be used to improve upon our prototypes.

In this sprint I also took the task of Scrum master upon myself. More on this can be read in the reflection.

Low-fidelity Prototype

As a group we decided to split up in three parts to each work on a different prototype. We chose to do this to let each group have their own perspective on the problem and to come up with a variety of solutions. I paired up with Wendy. We created an iPad/responsive website prototype. Testing

Now that the first prototype was finished, it was time to test it on our audience. Before we could do this it was essential to set up a test plan. This test plan describes what we are going to test, what metrics we are gonna record, the tasks the user needs to perform, and questions we ask during and after the testing. The full test plan for this prototype can be found here: here.
