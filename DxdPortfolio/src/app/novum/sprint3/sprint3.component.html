Sprint 3 - Concepting

Creating prototypes and testing them with our audience.

Introduction

Now that in the previous sprint we have more clearly defined the problems our audience faces, we can start working on coming up with concepts and prototypes. During this sprint we decided to focus on low-fidelity prototypes and test them with our audience. The insights we would get from these would be used to improve upon our prototypes.

In this sprint I also took the task of Scrum master upon myself. More on this can be read in the reflection.

We decided for everyone to make a concept for themselves to decide what each of us thought should be in the final version. Testing

Now that the first prototype was finished, it was time to test it on our audience. Before we could do this it was essential to set up a test plan. This test plan describes what we are going to test, what metrics we are gonna record, the tasks the user needs to perform, and questions we ask during and after the testing. The full test plan for this prototype can be found here: here.

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic we had quite some trouble finding suitable test-subjects. I let my grandfather of 74 years test the prototype. For this I recorded our conversation and his mouse clicks. The video of this test is in dutch and can be found below:


The summary of the test results goes as follows:

"The participant thought the prototype had a good logical flow, but he got confused at the uploading verification part. He also missed titles on pages, which led him to not know what the function of the page was. He did like the fact that you can select which organisations you are giving someone power of attorney to."

Reflection

I think this sprint was really nice because we actually got to think more creatively and start working on more tangible products.

However, there were some major issues in our group that came up during this sprint. Previously, Diederik had been Scrum master but felt he could not motivate the team well enough and had therefore stepped down this sprint. I decided I wanted to give the Scrum master role a try. In previous sprints Diederik did a lot of work that went beyond that of a Scrum master, he arranged a lot of meetings, communicated with the teachers, and tried to motivate us as a team. While this was good, I felt that a Scrum master was only responsible for the conducting of the daily stand-ups and retrospectives.

This lead to a conflict within our group. Some members thought that I was not doing enough for the group. Tensions grew high and it got a bit awkward among us. After discussing with the teachers what to do we decided to have a 360 feedback session. This was the first time I ever took part of a 360 feedback session.

In short: the feedback that I got was that they thought I was pleasant to work with, and that I did a lot of work, but I did not fulfill my duties as Scrum master. After this feedback session we decided to restructure our planning as most of us felt that the Scrum methodology was overkill for our project.
