Vega Collaboration

A collaboration with students from the South-African Vega School

Introduction

During the first weeks of my minor Digital Experience Design we got the oppurtunity to collaborate with the Johannesburg-based Vega School. Together with four other students, 2 who where from South-Africa, we worked on a project centering around transmedial world-building and storytelling. We were given the task to watch the documentary Before The Flood and build a transmedial world around it using three concepts. This page describes the process and our results of creating this world. Followed by a brief personal reflection on the assignment.

Research

During the first lessons we got paired with our teammates. Because of Covid-19 and the fact that we worked with students from South-Africa, it was logical to communicate and work together over Teams. The five of us got together and made some brief introductions. We set up our Google Drive and exchanged numbers to create a Whatsapp group. We quickly decided to each watch the documentary again and afterwards start analyzing together.

After we got back together we started analyzing the documentary and getting the most important information of the documentary in a single file. For us to create a successful transmedial campaign of Before the Flood, we needed get a proper understanding of the world and story the documentary was portraying. While watching the documentary we looked out for several things:

  • Locations: What locations where shown throughout the movie and why?
  • People: What people where presented, what did they talk about and add to the narrative?
  • Protagonist/Antagonist: Who or what was the hero of the story? And who or what was the villain.
  • Message: What is the overall message and goal of the documentary?

The results of our research document can be found here. This research would later help us find hooks in the original documentary to expand the transmedial universe. Along with this, the analysis helped us to start working on our campaign analysis.

For the campaign analysis we looked at the target audience we wanted to reach, created a problem statement, set up campaign goals, and started to think of concepts. The campaign analysis can be found here.

For each concept we tried to tie it in the Before the Flood universe and create something unique. We presented these concepts to our classmates and teachers in a presentation. The feedback we got from our teachers made clear that there were several problems with our concepts and our understanding of the assignment.

Primarily, our concepts lacked a proper story and an overarching connection. In addition to this, there were no clear protagonists and antagonists, and we should better think on how to involve the viewer.

Revised concepts

After the feedback we got from our teachers we decided to fix our concepts. We quickly figured out it was better to first start creating the overall story and then let the concepts flow naturally out from there.

Story and world: The story world is just like our real world, the difference is that the four elements, water, fire, earth and wind, are alive. They’ve let the humans live on earth peacefully for thousands of years, up until the humans started to use a more advanced technology which then started polluting the world more and more without knowing they were polluting it. The elementals were in a long sleep because they didn't have to worry about the world for centuries. But once they realized that the climate was changing rapidly, they woke up and started fighting back.

Characters:

  • Earth Elemental: The earth elemental is the leader of the four, he was the first one to notice what the humans were doing to the earth. He is very angry and started punishing people by forcing droughts and attacking villages with small earthlike elemental soldiers.
  • Water Elemental: The water elemental is very impulsive, she doesn’t think before she acts. But she can also be very forgiving when she realizes what she has done. She can control the water and make big waves that can flood villages and drown people.
  • Fire Elemental: The fire elemental doesn’t want to hurt people, she is very shy and doesn’t turn up as frequently as the other elemental. The other elementals try to force her to burn villages by making wildfires, but she only does so when other elementals ask her to do so. Which makes her loyal to her companions.
  • Air Elemental: The air elemental is sneaky, he can become almost invisible and is trying to playfully punish the people. He is forcing strong winds to annoy people, sometimes he is making storms to force people into their houses and he is trying to intoxicate the people by making dense smog into the city.
  • Humans: Humans are very smart creatures, but they only think about themselves as species, they’re very selfish. By creating smart technologies, infrastructure, buildings, and cities, they’re polluting the world more than ever without realizing it.

A detailed explanation of our story world, characters, timeline, etc. can be found in our final draft.

Using this more detailed story world it was easier to see what was missing in our previous concepts. We used this new information to revise our old concepts and come up with new ideas. The revised concepts can be found below:

Concept #1: The Netflix Show - The Flood
Title: The Flood
Story: The Introductions begins with a flood on the tropical island of Polynesia, with the character/protagonist Selina being separated from her friends and family due to the wave of the flooding. She is forced to survive on her own as the floodwaters continue to rise, she desperately searches for her tarp clearing the waste matters in the water and in doing so the water elemental notices her actions of removing waste from the water. Later as the second wave of the flood comes in from the shore, she and the water elemental realize she has nowhere to escape to. In the spur of the moment, the elemental decides to save her because of her actions. Selina now understands that cleaning up waste from the water makes the flooding go away. The elemental and she find her people together and Selina explains how they can make the water elemental less angry so he doesn’t cause any more flooding.
Narrative timeline: Present (2020)
Protagonist: Selina, a 16-year-old island girl
Antagonist: Water elemental

Concept #2: The Instagram Comic Book - SmogShot
Title: SmogShot
Story: The story starts with a photographer, Imae, trying to make nice pictures in the city. However, Imae realizes that the smog holds her back from making the pictures of an otherwise beautiful city. So she starts researching the cause of the smog and tries to capture this. The air elemental sees her effort and can also see the influence she has on the other inhabitants of the city. Because big industrial companies are hugely shamed and becoming bankrupt, the smog clears up. The elemental, hesitant at first, becomes more friendly and Imae captures the city in its natural beauty again.
Narrative timeline: Present (2020)
Protagonist: Imae, a 20-year-old photographer living in a very smog heavy city
Antagonist: Air elemental

Concept #3: The Single Player Game - Earthwake
Title: EarthWake
Story: You play with Miguel, who is an animal rescuer near the Amazon rainforest. There are loads of deforestation areas in the Amazon rainforest where helpless animals are fighting for survival. As Miguel, you want to save those animals, but the earth elemental gets in the way. The elemental sends his minions out there to fight you because he’s angry at the humans for destroying his forest. When the minions have almost killed you, you realize that fighting them won’t help those animals, nor you, so you decide to have a different approach: helping earth instead of fighting it. You discover that the elemental is very angry at lumberjacks because they’re the ones who are cutting down the trees and you help earth destroy those lumberjacks. It takes the earth elemental awhile to realize what you’ve been doing because the other elementals have shown up as well. But after some convincing from the other elementals, earth realizes that humans are fighting his fight and he gives the earth its soil back so that the Amazon rainforest can grow again.
Narrative timeline: Present (2020)
Protagonist: Miguel, an 18-year-old animal rescuer who lives near the Amazon rainforest
Antagonist: Earth elemental and lumberjacks

Reflection

I think the Vega collaboration worked as a great introduction to DXD for the first few weeks. As someone who had limited knowledge of design going into the minor, it was a great way to start experimenting with story creation and different kinds of media. In the beginning I was not sure what exactly was expected from us. But as time went on and I was able to speak with teachers on our work, it became more evident and more clear throughout the weeks.

The collaboration with the South-Africans was a unique experience. I noticed several different ways of approching work between us, the dutch students, and the south-africans. Some were practical such as that they often experienced electricity issues or internet connectivity issues that hindered our work. But I also noticed that the south-african students were more withholding during discussions. They were often more quiet and did most of the work on their own time when our Teams meetings had ended.

It was interesting to see these differences. I tried to be as open-minded as possible to overcome these differences so we could create a project we were proud of. I think we succeeded in this, and our teachers rewarded us with a collaboration award during the final assessment.
